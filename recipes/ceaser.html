DESCRIPTION

This classic Caesar salad recipe features crisp romaine lettuce, homemade croutons, and a rich, creamy dressing made from scratch. The combination of fresh garlic, anchovy paste, and Parmesan cheese creates that distinctive Caesar flavor that's impossible to resist.

While many modern versions skip the anchovy, this traditional recipe embraces it, creating a perfect balance of flavors that has made this salad a restaurant favorite for decades. The homemade croutons add a delicious crunch that store-bought varieties just can't match.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 large heads romaine lettuce
  • 1 cup high-quality olive oil
  • 3 cups day-old bread cubes
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon anchovy paste
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

STEPS

  1. Make croutons: Toss bread cubes with olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. Bake at 375Â°F until golden.
  2. Prepare dressing: Whisk egg yolks, garlic, anchovy paste, lemon juice, and mustard.
  3. Slowly drizzle in olive oil while whisking to emulsify the dressing.
  4. Wash and dry romaine leaves thoroughly, then tear into bite-sized pieces.
  5. Toss lettuce with dressing, adding half the Parmesan cheese.
  6. Top with croutons and remaining Parmesan cheese.
  7. Season with fresh ground black pepper and serve immediately.
